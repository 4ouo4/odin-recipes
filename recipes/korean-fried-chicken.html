Korean Soy Garlic Fried Chicken - Dakgangjeong

Highly addictive! They are coated with sweet, savory, and sticky soy garlic sauce.
Pair these with some pickled radish and you've got yourself a bomb ass meal.

Ingredients

  • Chicken & Seasonings
    • 2 lbs boneless chicken thigh fillets
    • 2 tbsp rice wine
    • 1 tsp onion powder
    • 1 tsp garlic powder
    • 1 tsp salt
    • 1/4 tsp ginger powder
    • a pinch of ground black pepper
  • Other
    • 1.5 cups corn starch
    • cooking oil
  • Soy Garlic Sauce
    • 1/4 cup soy sauce
    • 2 tbsp brown sugar
    • 2 tbsp honey
    • 2 tbsp water
    • 2 tbsp rice wine
    • 1/2 tbsp minced garlic
    • 1/2 tbsp minced ginger
    • a pinch of ground black pepper
    • 3 dried chilies (optional)
    • 2 green onions (optional)
  • Slurry
    • 2 tsp corn starch
    • 2 tsp water
  • Garnish
    • toasted sesame seeds
    • green onions, thinly sliced

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400F
  2. In a large bowl, place chopped chicken and add rice wine, onion powder, garlic powder, salt, garlic powder, and ground black pepper. Combine well. Set aside.
  3. Place corn starch in a large bowl. Dredge seasoned chicken pieces in corn starch and evenly coat them
  4. Place chicken pieces onto wire rack of baking sheet
  5. Spray some oil lightly over chicken pieces, to assist with better browning (optional)
  6. Cook for 10 minutes, then turn them over and spray oil lightly to cover dried white spots. Cook for another 10 minutes or until golden brown
  7. In a pan, combine all the sauce ingredients
  8. Bring sauce to a boil over medium low heat while stirring often
  9. Once sauce starts bubbling, take out chilies and green onion pieces
  10. In a separate bowl, combine and mix the slurry ingredients then pour over the sauce. Stir often
  11. Once sauce thickens (about 1-2 minutes), remove from the heat and cool down for 5 minutes
  12. Gently baste the chicken with the sauce
  13. Once all coated, garnish with sesame seeds and green onions. Enjoy!!
